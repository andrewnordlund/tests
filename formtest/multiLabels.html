Summary

  • Only SRs (Screen Readers) were tested. No vooice control systems were tested.
  • All tested SR+Browser combos support single <label>s, whether implicit or explicit.
  • Having two explicit <label>s does not work on iOS, or with Internet Explorer, or Safari+VoiceOver on OS X Catalina.
  • Having an <input> in the middle of text (ie: two implicit <label>s) works on everything except VoiceOver desktop + Safari.
  • Having two elements referenced by aria-labelledby works in everything except Internet Explorer in NVDA and Narrator.
  • You can get NVDA and Narrator to read the multiple aria-labelledby in IE by adding tabindex="-1" to the labelledby elements.

Conclusion

It's best to just have 1 <label>. But if you must have two: try to use 1 <label>, and reference the other with aria-describedby. If you can't do that, then use aria-labelledby unless you care about Internet Explorer....which you shouldn't. It's not 2004 anymore.

Or if you don't care about Mac users, then two implicit <label>s works.
